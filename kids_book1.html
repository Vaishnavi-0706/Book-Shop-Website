All You Need to Know About Children’s Books Online at Best Prices

The children's books category comprises a range of books across genres, offering captivating stories designed to entertain and educate young readers. These books play a crucial role in fostering a love for reading, sparking creativity, and imparting valuable life lessons to children.

Here we attempt to answer all the questions you may have about the children’s book category at Bookshop.com.

What are children’s books?

Children's books are specifically written and illustrated for young readers, typically ranging from infants to early teenagers. These books cover a wide range of genres, themes, and reading levels to cater to different age groups and developmental stages. They often feature engaging stories, colorful illustrations, and age-appropriate language to captivate the young readers and foster a love for reading. Children's books play a vital role in nurturing literacy skills, sparking creativity, and promoting emotional and cognitive development in children.

Who are the target readers for children’s books?

The general target reader categories for children's books are:

  • 0-3 years – Board Books: These books have sturdy pages that can be easily handled by toddlers. The content features rhyme and repeat lines. They usually have fewer than 20 pages and 300 words. Example: Baby Touch series
  • 3-8 years – Picture Books: These books feature rich illustrations and engaging stories, often read aloud by parents or caregivers. Picture books rely on illustrations to tell the story, frequently imparting valuable lessons centered around developing emotional intelligence, positive relationships, social connections, and moral values. Example: The Very Hungry Caterpillar by Eric Carle
  • 5-8 years – Early Readers: Designed for children who are starting to read independently. These books have short sentences and focus on simplicity and repetition of the text, rather than the story. This is primarily to encourage the child to read by deciphering the words. Example: The Gruffalo by Julia Donaldson
  • 7-10 years – Chapter Books: These books have more complex stories and language, and therefore, are broken into chapters to facilitate easy reading. They often feature illustrations. Example: Junie B Jones series
  • 8-12 years – Middle Grade Books: These books feature longer narratives, more complex themes, and age-appropriate content, often intended as a first step towards regular fiction reading. Example: Matilda by Roald Dahl
